The biggest galaxies

And curiosities about them.

IC 1101

IC 1101 is the largest galaxy in the universe. The galaxy is elliptical to lenticular and appears as a golden yellow color. The brightest galaxy is located more than 1.04 billion light-years from Earth.

Phoenix Cluster

Phoenix Cluster is one of the most massive galaxy groups in the universe. It is located about 5.7 billion light-years from Earth. The elliptical galaxy is composed of average and dark intra-group material.

NGC 262

NGC 262 is located 202 million light-years from Earth, which is one of the largest known spiral galaxies. The diameter of the galaxy is approximately 1.3 million light-years.

NGC 4889

NGC 4889 is also famous as "Coma B," an enormous elliptical galaxy located approximately 308 million light-years from Earth. It was also discovered by the British astronomer Frederick William Herschel I in 1785.

NGC 4874

NGC 4874, also known as "Coma A," was discovered in 1785 by the British astronomer Frederick William Herschel I. It is 350 million light-years away from Earth. It is a smooth, ball-shaped galaxy, surrounded by a stellar halo.

A2261-BCG

The A2261-BCG is located approximately 3 billion light-years from Earth. It is elliptical in shape and was discovered by the Hubble Space Telescope. The A2261-BCG has a diameter of one million light-years.
